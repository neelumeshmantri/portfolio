About Me

I am a passionate student researcher driven by curiosity and creative problem-solving, with interests spanning mathematics, computer science, and quantum computing. Through programs like RSI(C) at IIT Madras and PACT at Rutgers, I have developed advanced computational thinking, earning top national ranks in Bebras Computing Challenge and Technothlon by IIT Guwahati.

I seize every opportunity to learn, innovate, and make a meaningful difference.

Differential Heat Equation

Supervisor: Prof. Dr. Venkatabalaji TE

This report examines the derivation, solutions and various applications of the partial differential heat equation.

Key Components:

  • Derives the heat equation in n-dimensions
  • Shows solutions through two different methods: Fourier Series and Finite Difference Method
  • Includes Python code involving the Explicit method graphing temperature distribution over time
  • Exploring real life applications using Wolfram Mathematica: critically simulated, modelled and analyzed heat flow in various parts of an automobile
  • Modified the heat equation by adding the terms of advection and vorticity heating

AI-Powered Vaping Detection Device

Design Patent (India) — Under Review

This device controls the AI and CCTV sensors to detect vaping in real time. Once detected, the app will alert designated personnel such as school/college authorities and security teams to take immediate action. We parents are very much concerned about the wide prevalence of vaping in educational institutions like coaching centres, parties etc.

Children hide vapes (e-cigarettes) in their pockets and socks. I really wanted to find a solution and shared this new idea of developing an AI-based device. We are still developing an app which uses machine learning and AI technologies not to just alert but also identify the students vaping. The devices can be installed in the corridors of schools, colleges, restaurants, hotels, malls, and places which are frequented by youngsters.
